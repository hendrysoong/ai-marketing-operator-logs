## v3.3 — Two-Layer Output

**Date:** January 2026
**Type:** Feature
**Generation:** GEN 3

Generated 5 files and 3,000 words of competitive intelligence. Actionable insight was one sentence buried on page 4. Added synthesis phase with two-layer output: Brief (200 words) and Full Report (2,000+ words).

**Principle:** Comprehensive collection serves different purpose than decisive output. Separate them.

**Tags:** Listen-Competitors, Output Design

## v3.3 — Every Claim Must Have Source URL

**Date:** January 2026
**Type:** Feature
**Generation:** GEN 3

Found claims that couldn't be traced back to source. Added citation requirement: every claim must have a source URL.

**Principle:** Uncited claims are hallucination risks. Forced citation eliminates fabricated intelligence.

**Tags:** Listen-Competitors, Citations

## v3.1 — Early Synthesis Creates Confirmation Bias

**Date:** January 2026
**Type:** Failure
**Generation:** GEN 3

Synthesising findings during collection biased what was collected next. Agent looked for evidence supporting early conclusions.

**Principle:** Early synthesis creates confirmation bias. Collect everything first, then synthesise.

**Tags:** Listen-Competitors, Bias, Failure

## v3.1 — Two-Phase Search

**Date:** January 2026
**Type:** Learning
**Generation:** GEN 3

Split search into two phases: broad discovery (cast wide net) then focused deep-dive (investigate specific signals).

**Principle:** Search in their language, not yours. Customers don't use your product categories.

**Tags:** Listen-Competitors, Search, Learning

## v1.0.1 — HUNT/MONITOR Dual-Mode Architecture

**Date:** January 2026
**Type:** Architecture
**Generation:** GEN 2

Two modes: HUNT (active competitive research on a specific competitor) and MONITOR (ongoing monitoring of competitive landscape).

**Principle:** Design around constraints. Two modes for two purposes.

**Tags:** Listen-Competitors, Architecture
